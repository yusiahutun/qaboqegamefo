Frosted eyeshadow is back heres how to wear it now

Publish date: 2024-03-28

There’s no denying that Noughties nostalgia is dominating fashion this season: peplum tops, mesh ballet flats and cargo pants have all segued from the runways to the streets. But when it comes to the beauty return cropping up on everyone’s mood boards, one key player is proving most popular. Yes, frosted eyeshadow is back in business.

Chanel made the call at its autumn/winter 2023 Paris fashion Week show, sending models out with shimmery, silver-grey toned eye make-up signalling a stark contrast to seasons (if not years) of warm, neutral shades dominating make-up aesthetics.

Stephane Cardinale - Corbis//Getty Images

Frosted eyeshadow on Chanel’s autumn/winter 2023 runway

And now, Charlotte Tilbury has confirmed it the look of the season, choosing her muses Kate Moss and Jourdan Dunn to front the brand’s new holiday campaign imagery with high voltage frosted smoky eye make-up.

Courtesy of Charlotte Tilbury

Jourdan Dunn stars alongside Kate Moss in Charlotte Tilbury's holiday campaign

The resurgence, according to make-up artist and MAC ambassador Dominic Skinner, can be attributed to this decade's Euphoria-esque energy: "The sparkle, the individuality, the statement – it all plays a part in the empowerment of wearing something bold and fun," he says. When looking back to look forwards, think "less Britney and more of an Xtina attitude", he offers.

Sofia Tilbury, artistry director for Charlotte Tilbury, was behind Moss’ make-up for the brand's shoot, telling Bazaar that a key reference for the look was the supermodel’s iconic “gilded glamour” at the 2009 Met Gala, “where the feline shape of her eyes was accentuated with cool-toned winged eyeshadow and sparkling, eye-widening pops of shimmer”. On a 2023 Moss, Tilbury used the brand’s new Beautyverse Palette, with the Crystal Glow and Party Topper shades Cyber Silver and Beautyverse “pressed onto Kate’s lids to catch the light and create that glam-rock glow”.

Naturally, these model looks (via Charlotte Tilbury and Chanel) give off a very current vibe as opposed to ‘Y2K school prom’. Key here is the placement – stay away from the brow bones – and the textures used. Take note: “The challenge with the frosted look is finding the right balance of textures and colours to create a bold, shimmering yet harmonious eye look,” Tilbury tells us. “You want frosted eyes to look wide, bright and glossy rather than powdery, dull or texture-enhancing.”

Kevin Mazur/MG23//Getty Images

Gigi Hadid and Lizzo with frosted eye shadow at the 2023 Met Gala

She says to “look for eyeshadows that have a silky application and high-colour pay-off, then start with finely milled, neutral mattes to contour the eye shape and create a sculpted under-layer before working your way up to adding icy silvers and blues on the inner corners and centres of the lids”.

If such cool tones don’t work well with your colourings (or push you too far from your comfort zone), know that Tilbury likes to add warmth by layering pink, chocolate and bronze shades “into the crease and outer corners of the eyes, blending out into a winged shape that widens and lifts while helping emphasise those cooler, more futuristic finishes”. Reserve the shimmer for the lower lash line, centre of the lid and inner corners of the eyes for a wide-eyed pop “and you’ll have an easy, show-stopping festive eye in seconds”, she says.

And to maintain the modernity elsewhere with your make-up, Tilbury recommends pairing frosted eyeshadow with dewy, glowing skin (as opposed to the flat, matte complexions we favoured in the Nineties and Noughties). This, plus “blushed pink cheeks” and “polished, glossy lips” is the formula we’ll rely on when executing its comeback. Pass the palette…
