Top 10 weirdest European foods that only the brave can eat

Publish date: 2024-09-23

As you explore and discover new cultures, you are likely to come across ideas, foods and lifestyles above your realm of what is considered normal. One of the things that might take you by surprise are the weirdest European foods. So, do you know any of these foods?

You may have probably heard stories about European eating the weirdest delicacy. If yes, have you asked yourself whether it is true or false, or which kind of food they are? There are several unusual European foods to consider and perhaps dig into if you are curious.

Weird eastern European food

Different continents have dishes that distinguish them from others, and Europe is no exception. When you talk about European cuisine, most people think of rich comfort foods. However, as in the case with many other cuisines, even the Europeans have a good number of weird foods. Here is a list of some gross European foods only the brave can eat.

1. Maggots cheese

Maggot cheese, also known as casu marzu, is a traditional Sardinian sheep milk cheese that contains live insect larvae or maggots. Casu marzu is made by going beyond the standard phase of fermentation until the cheese is dissolved, starting with the manufacture of Pecorino cheese.

The pecorino cheese is usually left outside for flies to deposit their eggs in it. Once the eggs open, the maggots larvae start feeding on the cheese, and the cheese is ready. Unfortunately, the Piophilidae cheese fly larvae are reintroduced to the cheese after it has decomposed, rendering it unsafe to consume.

Even though European Union food relegations have officially made casu marzu (maggot cheese) illegal, it is still available on the black market, where it may be sold for double the original price.

2. Frog legs

They are one of the renowned delicacies of French cuisine, and it has been considered a national delicacy. Frog legs are very nutritious as they are rich in protein, omega-3 fatty acids, vitamin A, and potassium. They are served sautéed in garlic butter sauce as an appetizer.

They taste like chicken because of their mild flavour, with a texture similar to chicken wings. Although frog legs are common in French cuisine, they are also consumed in many parts of the world, including China, Thailand, the Caribbean, Vietnam, and Indonesia.

3. Head cheese

If you have never seen this type of food, you might think it's some variety of fancy cheese. However, the food has nothing to do with cheese but rather a pig's head. Head cheese is a cold-cut terrine or meat jelly made with flesh from the head of a calf or pig.

It is often eaten cold, at room temperature, or in a sandwich. This type of food is unique for its various textures ranging from crunchy ears to savoury gelatin and creamy pig fat.

4. Morcilla blood sausage

Morcilla blood sausage is one of the grossest European foods and one of the world's strangest and most exotic foods. This dish is commonly found in Spain. The dish consists of cooked pork blood mixed with rice and onions and looks like a normal sausage, but it is dark in colour. The texture of the blood sausage is soft, almost creamy.

5. Salo

Salo is a famous traditional Ukrainian food made of cured pork fatback or boneless pork belly, with or without skin. Salo is mostly fat and can be salted or fermented in brine, treated with condiments or smoked.

In some countries, it is served as an appetizer, together with vodka. Some people believe that if you keep eating Salo while drinking, you will never get drunk, and you can have as much vodka as you want.

6. OKroshka

This is a cold soup of Russian origin. The soup is a mixture of mostly raw vegetables, boiled potatoes, eggs, and cooked meat like beef, veal, sausages and kvass. Kvass is a fermented low-alcoholic beverage made from rye bread.

It is always served cold and considered the best way to fight the summer heat. Some people associate this food with traditional Russian salads, where every ingredient is chopped into small, almost equal pieces.

7. Svið

The habit of cooking the animal's head is not strange to some European countries, even though it is very common in African countries. Svið is a traditional Icelandic dish consisting of lamb or a sheep's head cut in half, boiled or oven-roasted with the brain removed.

It is one of the grossest food in the world, and it is sometimes cooked with onions, carrots and different herbs to give it flavour and is sometimes cured in lactic acid.

8. Caraway seed soup

You probably heard about Caraway seed soup. This is a dish that requires very few ingredients, such as chicken broth, flour and caraway seeds and is commonly found in Slovak and Hungarian cuisines.

The cost of preparing this food is very low as it can be prepared for less than $1 if you use water instead of chicken broth. This dish is normally served with croutons or dumplings.

9. Escargots snails

Escargots snails are one of the unique European foods usually served as an appetizer. They are commonly found in France and are known for being low in fat and high in water and proteins. Escargots is a typical dish in the region of Burgundy, but it is also found in other places in France.

10. Cold borscht

It's a national Ukrainian soup consisting of beetroot, beef and other vegetables and herbs. While some find cold borscht weird, others find it fascinating. This soup can be served with a boiled egg, sour cream and fresh dill. Sometimes they put boiled or fried potato as a side.

There are several weirdest European foods that it's hard to explain if they are actually food. These foods include escargots snails, maggot cheese and Morcilla blood sausage. While foreigners find these foods weird, Europeans find them fascinating and delicious.
