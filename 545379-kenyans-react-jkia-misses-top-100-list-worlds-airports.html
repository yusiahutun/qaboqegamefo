Kenyans React as JKIA Misses Out on Top 100 List of World's Best Airports

Publish date: 2024-08-11
  • Doha’s Hamad International Airport was named the World’s Best Airport 2024 in the World Airport Awards announced at the Passenger Terminal EXPO
  • Cape Town emerged as the top-ranked airport in Africa with Johannesburg claiming third place and Nairobi securing the 10th position
  • Kenyans expressed concerns following the ranking as some stated that Nairobi did not deserve the 10th position

Nancy Odindo, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings more than three years of experience covering politics, news, and feature stories across digital and print media in Kenya.

Jomo Kenyatta International Airport's condition has been a topic of discussion for several months.

Kipchumba Murkomen, the Transport and Roads Cabinet Secretary, acknowledged the issues, attributing leaky roofs and poor drainage to former President Uhuru Kenyatta's administration.

Meanwhile, Doha’s Hamad International Airport claimed the title of World’s Best Airport 2024 in the World Airport Awards, announced at the Passenger Terminal EXPO in Frankfurt on April 17, 2024.

Singapore Changi Airport secured second place, with Seoul Incheon Airport following closely in third.

In Africa, Cape Town emerged as the top-ranked airport, with Johannesburg claiming third place and Nairobi securing the 10th position.

Globally, only three African airports made it to the top 100 list (Cape Town, Durban King Shaka, and Johannesburg), according to Skytrax.
