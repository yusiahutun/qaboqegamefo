15 best Nigerian tribal tattoo ideas and their meanings

Publish date: 2024-06-27

African tribal symbols are distinctive due to their ties to tradition and culture. These inscriptions were traditionally used for medicinal purposes or as a status symbol within the clan or community. Nigeria is no exception, as various tribes use symbols to represent their heritage. With such a rich and diverse history, it's no surprise that tribal tattoos have become popular. So if you consider getting a Nigerian tribal tattoo, which one should you choose? You'll be glad to know that various designs exist, each with a specific meaning.

If you like body art, you've probably seen various Nigerian tribal tattoo designs. These designs are one-of-a-kind and have special meaning in the communities where they originated. These tribal designs are timeless because they represent a collective artistic heritage. Tribes have traditionally used these symbols to describe rites of passage, significant accomplishments, status, and protection.

Nigeria is a diverse country with over 250 ethnic tribes. These tribes have contributed to the country's culture, tradition and diversity. One common thing with these tribes is using body and facial markings to mark various societal accomplishments.

As a result, tribal designs and symbols have gained popularity due to their uniqueness and significance. So, if you are interested in Nigerian tattoos and meanings, the ones listed below should appeal to you.

1. Kolo tribal tattoo

Kolo tribal body art is a popular design among the Yoruba people. Historically, the Yoruba of Nigeria marked the body with extensive scar tattoos known as kolo. These markings are part of the Yoruba tattoos and symbolise courage and beautification. The symbols were prevalent among women who acquired them before marriage and childbirth.

2. Heena/Lalle

Henna or Lalle is a popular tribal design from the northern parts of Nigeria. This design is associated with Muslim and Indian weddings but is also prevalent among the northern tribes of Nigeria. Henna dye marks on the hands, palms and feet in many Hausa and Fulani weddings symbolise beauty, purity, rebirth and youth.

3. Adinkra tribal designs

The Sankofa Andikra symbol originates from the western parts of Africa. The Sankofa symbol is a bird with an egg in her mouth, turning backwards. The design symbolises forgiveness, positivity, mistakes and the future.

4. Oshun goddess design

The Oshun river goddess is an ideal choice if you are looking for a Nigerian warrior tattoo. Oshun is the Yoruba river goddess. African warriors believed in numerous gods and goddesses to give them the power to fight during wars and win. The Oshun tribal motif symbolises life, rebirth, strength, power, courage and divinity.

5. Nigerian elephant tattoo

The elephant is a symbol of strength in many African communities. Nigerian tribes are no exception, as they also acknowledge the might of the mammal by getting tattoos to symbolise power. The Nigerian elephant motif represents strength, power and wisdom.

6. Ichi facial tattoos

If you are looking for Nigerian Igbo tattoos, the Ichi tribal design is one of them. Ichi tribal designs originate from the men of the Igbo tribe. They represent nobility, pride and respect. Ichi inscriptions are essential to the Igbo tribespeople and are respected members of the society.

The two types of Ichi designs include Agbaja, distinguished by their moon-like circles and semicircles, and Ndri design, known by sun-like carved lines from the forehead to the chin. The Ichi design is ideal if you fancy getting one of these Nigerian tattoo symbols.

7. Bini “blade” (Iwu) tattoos

If you were looking for Nigerian tribal tattoos with meaning, the Iwu is an excellent design. Bini or Edo are people from southwest Nigeria. They traditionally practice a tattooing method known as the Iwu. The Iwu, drawn by an Owisu (one who sculpts tattoos), symbolises pride and strength.

8. Ila tribal/face tattoos

The Ila face tattoo is popular among the Yoruba tribe from western Nigeria. Ila is a unique mark drawn, Okola, on the face or body of an individual shortly after birth or during childhood. The Ila face tattoos symbolise beauty, identity, and health or are used to perform certain traditional rites.

9. Fulani facial tattoos

The Fulani facial tattoo is among the popular traditional Nigerian tattoos. The design includes a black circle on the mouth. The Fulani mark their mouths with black dye to signify beauty. They also represent a rite of passage from childhood to adulthood. Fulani tribal tattoos symbolise fertility, beauty and commitment.

10. Woodabe facial tattoos

The Wodaabe are a subgroup of the Fulani ethnic group. Most of the Wodaabe have their faces tattooed. They use razors to cut their faces with charcoal to mark the site. The modifications occur at a young age, with differing subgroups having different patterns on their faces. Wodaabe women dot their temples, cheeks and lips with geometric tattoos to ward off evil spirits and symbolise beauty.

11. Uli's tribal tattoos

Uli is a drawing made on the skin with dark and light dyes, usually yellow, black, white, or brown. Their primary meaning is to make a woman beautiful and ready for tribe holidays. Uri or Uli tribal marks are temporary lines that create multiple shapes on female bodies, hands, and legs lasting approximately seven days. Uri tribal tattoos represent royalty, beauty and culture.

12. Funtu design

Funtu is a West African symbol for Siamese crocodiles. The Funtu symbolises unity despite facing different directions or wanting different things. The design emphasises the value of individual identity, how it should not be compromised, and how one should respect the uniqueness of others.

13. Kada Mai ban Tsoro (crocodile)

This ink, Kada Mai ban Tsoro in Hausa, represents the "dangerous crocodile." The Kada represents a desire for power, deception, and mischief and is not particularly positive. The Hausa crocodile design is ideal for popular ideas.

14. Oya (African goddess) tattoo

African warriors prayed to various gods and goddesses for strength to fight and win. Oya is an African deity revered by the Yoruba tribe in Nigeria, known for her ferocity and attentiveness. Oya is a powerful goddess capable of causing thunderstorms and hurricanes. The Oya design signifies strength, power and vigilance.

15. Epa mask tribal design

An Epa mask is a ceremonial mask Nigerian Yoruba wore during the Epa masquerade. An Epa tattoo design has deep meaning and represents a form of transformation or acquiring a new entity. The Yoruba people believed that wearing masks could influence or appeal to spirits.

It could be because Nigeria is home to over 200 tribes, and people who feel distanced from their heritage want to honour their roots using these designs.
