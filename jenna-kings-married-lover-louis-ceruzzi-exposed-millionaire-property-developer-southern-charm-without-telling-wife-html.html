As Jenna King proudly introduced her Southern Charm co-stars to her older, wealthy lover, plying them with wine and a sensational view from a swanky New York penthouse, she simply referred to him as 'Lou'.

But the mysterious Lou's surname was never revealed on camera, nor was the exact nature of his relationship with the spiky-haired reality TV star.

And for good reason, too, as today MailOnline can exclusively reveal that Jenna's secret boyfriend is MARRIED real estate developer Louis Ceruzzi, and his wife - who agrees that Jenna is a 'gold digger' - knew NOTHING about his appearance on the show.

Friends have revealed that although Terese knew her husband had dalliances with other women, she never thought he would embarrass her by appearing on such a show.

Indeed, Ceruzzi only agreed to filming as 'fame hungry' Jenna asked him - and he never thought the show would even air, a friend said.

And despite being tracked down to his sprawling estate in Southport, Connecticut, father-of-two Ceruzzi - who also bought a $8 million home in Nantucket, Massachusetts, in 2004 - insisted he is not mega-wealthy and was never Jenna's 'sugar daddy', as believed.

Ceruzzi, who is 60 and NOT 55 as claimed on the show, told MailOnline in an exclusive interview he never even owned the $8 million penthouse on Bond Street, Nolita, where Jenna and her co-stars were filmed sipping drinks in the sunshine, insisting he does not own any property in Manhattan.

Ceruzzi said: ‘I would say I’m successful, but I had no idea I would be portrayed in that fashion. I guess I should have known - it’s reality TV - but I was not happy about it.’

Despite his protestations, Ceruzzi's company, Starwood Ceruzzi, works on large-scale projects, which have included a Linden, New Jersey, shopping center that sold for $54.5 million in 2003, and he’s an investor in upscale Manhattan restaurants Bottega del Vino, Sfoglia and Via Quadronno.

And he was also was named by his local Fairfield, CT, newspaper as owning a Bentley listed as one of the top-50 highly assessed vehicles in the county.

On the show, Jenna swore she didn’t care about the things her boyfriend’s cash could buy, promising that if that were the case she 'would have married Lou seven years ago’ and that ‘it’s not about money for me at all.'

But she couldn't have married Lou, since he was already married.

It is clear that Ceruzzi did spend a vacation with Jenna and her mother, Jane Shirer, in Italy in 2011, with Jane posting pictures of the couple - before Ceruzzi had a middle-aged make-over and shaved his head- alongside pictures of the yacht they sailed on and the villa where they stayed.

Ceruzzi's wife of 35 years was nowhere to be seen.

And when asked about her husband's relationship with the leopard-print-loving social climber, Terese Ceruzzi, 64, told MailOnline that she had never watched Southern Charm, before admitting: 'I did hear about this show.'

Confessing she is familiar with Jenna's name, Terese said she tries 'not to judge people anymore', but when quizzed as to whether she would call Jenna a gold digger, she replied: 'I would probably say so.'

Ceruzzi added: ‘I haven't spoken with Jenna in over six months', while Jenna is now happily dating Falling in Reverse rocker Ronnie Radke.

Claiming that 'I do investment stuff', Whitney admitted: 'Investments is a bit sketchy, I'm assuming her boyfriend helps out with her rent. I wish I had a rich boyfriend paying my way through life,' as Jenna told the cameras: 'It's not anybody's business as to how I pay my way.'

However, MailOnline told in April how the house was rented just for the production, according to a local realtor, who said: 'They used it for some interior and exterior shots and Jenna had absolutely nothing else to do with the property. She never lived there or owned it.’

And indeed, more of the show's carefully constructed sham came undone when it was revealed that Jenna’s last real residence in Charleston was a simple one-floor, three-bedroom rental on Saint Margaret Street that she shared in 2007 with other students while attending Trident Technical College in North Charleston.
