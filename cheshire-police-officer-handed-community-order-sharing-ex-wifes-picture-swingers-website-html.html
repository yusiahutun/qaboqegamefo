Cheshire police officer shared ex-wife's picture on SWINGERS website

Publish date: 2024-06-11

A police officer has been handed a community order after he shared his ex-wife's pictures on a swingers website.

PC Alan Friday, 42, went on to bombard Andrea Church, her twin sister and her sister's husband with 50 phone calls begging them not to report him.

At Wigan Magistrates' Court, Friday was found guilty of harassment and banned from contacting Ms Church under the terms of a two year restraining order. He now faces a disciplinary hearing after being suspended from his job last year.

After his former spouse found out her picture had been shared on the website, the dog handler for Cheshire Police rang her and confessed: 'I have done something wrong, I am going to lose my job and the dogs will be taken off me.'

But when she told him not to contact her again and fled to her twin sister Hayley's house, Friday turned up unannounced on the doorstep to 'explain.'

He repeatedly called the two women and emailed Ms Church to beg her not to report him.

Friday was later arrested at his home in Halewood, near Liverpool. The court heard the couple had been together for 11 years and married in 2013 before separating in June 2016.

Prosecutor Mr Chris Maloney said: 'Towards the end of that year the defendant used dating website FabSwingers to send a private image of her to another male user without her knowledge.

'The website connects users in close proximity and a friend of the victim was also using that website and they exchanged messages. He sent the image during this exchange. The family friend then recognised the person in the picture and the victim soon found out about it.

'She confronted the defendant about using her pictures and he said he would not do it again. Between this incident and the divorce there was contact between them which was sometimes friendly.

'However on 13 December 2019 the same family friend was using FabSwingers again. 

'He began a conversation and used an image of Andrea from a Facebook profile without her permission. The family friend realised it was likely to be this defendant and during the conversation called out the defendant saying he was "PC Plod A Fry".'

Friday emailed Ms Church at 14.55pm on December 14 to ask her not to report him after she stopped answering his phone calls. She replied: 'Do not even try to contact me again, you have issues you need to sort'. 

Mr Maloney added: 'He then sent further emails and she replied to one saying not to contact her again. At 15.06 she emailed saying she was going to the police.

'The defendant then drove to her twin sister Hayley's house and tried to explain why he had used that website. He would knock on the door without getting an answer again and again. 

'Between 15.11 and 19.08 on December 14 he bombarded Andrea with 24 calls, Hayley with eight calls and Hayley's partner with 20 calls - making 50 in total.'

Police arrested Friday at his home and took an Apple laptop and iPhone during a search of the house. 

The prosecutor said: 'He was interviewed the next day and replied no comment. He replied no comment at another interview on 15 May 2020.

'The prosecution say he harassed her during this period for the sole purpose of stopping her telling the police because she suspected he had committed an offence. He was a serving Cheshire Police officer working as a dog handler at the time.'

In mitigation for Friday, who denied the charge, defence lawyer Julian Goode said: 'He shared that image via SnapChat which is private and self-deleting. There is no evidence of psychological difficulties and distress for the victim. She has carried on going to the same gym as the defendant regularly.

'He is described by colleagues as a kind-hearted, genuine and absolutely selfless, and someone they would be proud to be associated with.'

But sentencing District Judge Mark Hadfield told Friday: 'You turned up at your ex-wife's sister's house on the basis that you would have known or believed your ex-wife was likely to have gone there for comfort and solace.

'Your ex-wife was left extremely distressed when she gave evidence and it is entirely understandable why she would have felt that way.'

Friday was made subject to a two-year community order with 20 rehabilitation requirement days and 100 hours unpaid work. 

He was also ordered to complete a Building Better Relationships programme and pay £710 in costs and surcharges. His iPhone and laptop was ordered to be forfeited and destroyed.

Friday was suspended from his policing duties last year. Cheshire Police said: 'We are aware of the outcome of the case involving Alan Friday, who has been convicted of harassment. It would be inappropriate for us to comment further due ongoing misconduct proceedings. Friday remains suspended from duty at the current time.'
