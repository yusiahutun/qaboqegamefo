Jerry Lee Lewis sues his daughter for alleged abuse

Publish date: 2024-04-03

Jerry Lee Lewis has filed a lawsuit against his daughter Phoebe Lewis claiming she abused him during her 12 years managing his career. 

The legendary rock n' roll star alleges Phoebe would serve him 'cocktails of psychotropic drugs' to control him, TMZ reported. 

The Great Balls of Fire singer also accused his daughter of keeping him in a moldy house where he was forced to wear an oxygen mask.

Jerry Lee Lewis, 81,  has filed a lawsuit against his daughter Phoebe Lewis, 53 (pictured in 2005) and her husband Ezekiel Loftin

Loftin is the husband of Lewis's daughter Phoebe, who is one of Lewis's six children, who he had with multiple women

In addition to the alleged abuse, the court documents state that Phoebe and her husband Ezekiel Loftin were reportedly after the 81-year-old's fortune, as he claims they blew $5million of his money. 

Lewis is reported to have a net worth of $15million according to TheRichest.com.  

While working as his manager between 2000 and 2012, his 53-year-old daughter allegedly forced him to go on grueling tours even while he was in poor health.
