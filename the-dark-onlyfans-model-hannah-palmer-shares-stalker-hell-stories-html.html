The dark side of OnlyFans: US model Hannah Palmer shares 'stalker from hell' stories

Publish date: 2024-06-16

A young model and OnlyFans star has been left feeling 'violated and unsafe' after a string of horrendous stalker encounters. 

Hannah Palmer joined the adults-only platform OnlyFans in March 2021 and claims to have made $5million since then. But the wealth and job of being in the public eye has come with some serious downsides regarding her privacy. 

The 24-year-old from the US, who boasts 2.2million Instagram followers, told FEMAIL a stalker followed her 'everywhere', broke into her mother's house demanding to meet her, recorded her in her apartment and posted the footage online.

It got to the point where she needed to call the FBI on one occasion and bought a second phone in an attempt to keep her number hidden from strangers. 

'I've hired a company to help manage my cyber security, which has helped me feel safer and more protected,' Hannah told Daily Mail Australia.

The model's very first encounter with a stalker took place in 2019 in her Los Angeles apartment without her knowing.

Hannah said the 'worst' stalker experience she's ever had occurred shortly after she moved into a new home last year. 

'I started receiving texts from an unknown number, including pictures of my house taken from their (the neighbour's) backyard, pictures of the inside of my house from Zillow, and disgusting verbal harassment,' she claims.  

'He also made threats, both violent and sexual, to myself and my family.' 

She contacted the police who came to the house to conduct a full report, which Hannah said was filed. 

'Then I had to file an injunction in court in order to take legal safety. So there's now a restraining order against them due to the violent and sexual threats, in fear that they may some day come to my house. Nothing has happened since then,' she said.
