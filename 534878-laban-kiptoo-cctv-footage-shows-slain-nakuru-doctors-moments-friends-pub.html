Laban Kiptoo: CCTV Footage Shows Slain Nakuru Doctor's Last Moments With Friends Inside Pub

Publish date: 2024-02-14
  • Laban Kiptoo's body was discovered in a ditch on Saturday, January 13, outside the Nakuru County Referral and Teaching Hospital
  • The 26-year-old had been revelling at a pub outside the hospital along the Nakuru-Kabarnet Road when he was last seen
  • The hospital's surveillance that would have captured the happenings at the trench was interfered with

Nakuru - The last moments of Laban Kiptoo have been traced to CCTV footage from a pub where he was revelling alongside his friends.

The 26-year-old's body was discovered inside a ditch outside the Nakuru County Referral and Teaching Hospital where he interned.

He spent his last moments at the joint in London Street along the Nakuru-Kabarnet Highway.

What did surveillance videos capture?

Exclusive footage published by NTV shows that the now-deceased seemed busy and moved around the establishment while interacting with his friends.

At the dawn of Saturday, January 13, a Kiptoo full of life walks around the pub as he alternates the people he interacts with therein.

All the while, he converses with someone on the phone as part of his company in the joint leaves.

He leaves alone a few moments later alone, this being his last time seen alive.

"It was at night when he joined his friends. He bought them a bottle and stayed around for some minutes before leaving. Later on, his friends came back after leaving," a staffer at the pub confided in NTV.

Meanwhile, the camera surveillance outside the hospital -that might have recorded the events at the scene where the body was found- was tampered with

The postmortem conducted on the body revealed that the medical intern died of manual strangultion.

Addressing the media, government pathologist Titus Ngulungu said the body had injuries on the head and part of the abdomen, as well as bruises on his wrists and lips.

“As a result of my examination I have formed the opinion that the cause of death was asphyxia due to compression of the neck,” said Ngulungu. The pathologist also ruled out the possibility of the deceased drowning, as he was found in a trench.

Kiptoo's tragic end left his kin and colleagues heartbroken as they came out to demand justice.

His sister learnt of the tragedy via social media.

"Why am I finding out through the internet that My brother is dead?"

What KMPDU said about Laban Kiptoo's death

The Kenya Medical Practitioners, Pharmacists, and Dentists Union (KMPDU) called for speedy investigations into the death.

In a statement on Sunday, January 14, KMPDU secretary-general Davji Atellah called on the DCI to move with speed and establish the cause of his death.

"We implore the relevant authorities to expedite the process, ensuring that justice is served swiftly. "We demand that county governments and hospital management prioritise the safety of healthcare workers, particularly medical interns," Atellah said.

What you need to know about Laban Kiptoo and tragedy that befell him

  • The 26-year-old was an intern at the Margaret Kenyatta Mother-Baby Maternity wing of the Nakuru county referral hospital.
  • He was with his friends on the night of Friday, January 12, revelling at a Nakuru nightclub near his station of work.
  • Kiptoo's body was discovered in a ditch near the entrance of the hospital, with visible injuries in the head.
  • A report of the autopsy conducted on the body indicated he died after manual strangultion.
