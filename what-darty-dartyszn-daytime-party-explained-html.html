What is a Darty? #Dartyszn and Daytime party explained

Publish date: 2024-09-04

In an age when people eat Tide Pods because they bear a passing resemblance to sweets, it shouldn’t come as a surprise that concepts like 'darty' and #dartyszn exist.

A portmanteau of ‘day’ and ‘party’, the concept seems to skip over the stigma surrounding consumption of alcohol during the daytime as easily as if it were skipping over the Alan Jackson Jimmy Buffett day drinking anthem, It’s Five O’Clock Somewhere.

Darty meaning

Much like the term ‘brunch’ fuses the best of both, breakfast and dinner, ‘darty’ fuses the concepts of party and daytime.

The 'darty' Urban Dictionary definition is: ‘A drunken party when the sun is out; a day time party.’

What is #dartyszn? 

The hashtag #dartyszn - meaning darty season - refers to the time at university towards the end of the semester when fraternities and sororities throw daytime weekend parties in their front yards and gardens.

The #dartyszn, popular on Instagram and Snapchat, supposedly refers more to spring and summer semesters, given the warmer weather, however, recently the darty season seems to apply to any time of year. It seems the darty never stops.

In an interview with the University of Alabama paper, the Crimson White, a sophomore explains the logic behind a darty: 'In the spring there aren’t any football games on Saturdays so darties are a great way to fill that void.

'People are excited about the nice weather again and want to spend time outside. What better way to do this than throwing a party?' 

Given that daytime is an integral part of a darty, it’s not surprising that most darties end by the time the sun goes down. 

As Flyby, Harvard Crimson blog, observes, to darty or to party seems to be the question: 'It’s very hard to rally once the alcohol and adrenaline wears off, so the choice to darty is the choice to not party that night.'

Where did #dartyszn come from?

The actual 'darty' definition first appeared on Urban Dictionary in early 2008, but the concept has gained more traction in recent years, with several American university publications making references to it.

A HuffPost article from 2012 refers to a darty as a daytime party that takes place when the weather gets hotter, but ends right before finals. 

The satirical Stanford paper, the Stanford Flipside, mocked the concept in an article entitled ‘Darty Season Bittersweet For Day-Drinking Alcoholic’.

Tips for Darty and #dartyszn

Since it’s a daytime party, sunscreen and glasses are an absolute must, says Her Campus, a media brand for college women.

If drinking is going to be involved, it is essential to have transport arrangements to and from the party set up prior to the darty.

It wouldn’t be a college darty if there wasn’t an element of competitiveness about it. Instagram is rife #dartyszn photos and videos in which hundreds of people watch a partygoer smash beers over his face on a rooftop, or a shirtless man diving into snow. The hashtag is also very popular on Snapchat.

Socialising plays a bigger part at a darty than at a party. Since it’s a party during the daytime in an open space, it’s easier to meet new people.

Kallen Sebastian, another interviewee from the Crimson White article said: “I feel like darties are more about who you’re with rather than where you’re going,' 

‘There’s also a larger variety of people looking to make new friends, so it’s not like the guys at the fraternities have dates or everyone is with a set group of people like it usually is when you go out at night.’ she added.

A daytime party is a good time to play games, an Ohio State University Odyssey post says: 'Start off with a few rounds of Kings, followed with Thumper, and then play the inevitable college game of beer pong.' 

The student newspaper of the American International University, The Yellow Jacket, also emphasises the need for darty games: 'Nothing says Darty like a game of cornhole, spikeball, ladder ball, wiffleball, kan jam.' 

It also stresses the importance of good music, to keep the party vibes going.
