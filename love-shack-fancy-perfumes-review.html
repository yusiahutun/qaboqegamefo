Moondance, Forever in Love, Boheme Review 2024

Publish date: 2024-03-09

Flowers are at the heart of the LoveShackFancy brand. If you’ve ever casually strolled past one of the brand’s 17 stores, you already know this to be true. Ditsy floral curtains drape the store’s windows, and branches adorned with pink and white petals burst out the door and across the building’s facade. Inside, slipdresses, gowns, ruffle skirts, and so much more are done in the brand’s signature floral prints.

Courtesy of LoveShackFancy

A world that founder and creative director Rebecca Hessel Cohen built on flowers—and pearls, bows, and tulle—is the ideal starting point for a perfume. Now, to kick off LoveShackFancy’s 10th anniversary, Hessel Cohen and her team are finally staking a claim in beauty with three Sephora-exclusive fragrances. It’s been a long time coming.

“Everyone’s like, ‘Fragrance, fragrance—when are you doing fragrance?’ for all those years—and my answer was always ‘Definitely sometime soon,’ but it was never the right moment,” Hessel Cohen tells Bazaar. “When we really said, ‘Okay, this is the time,’ we started to conceptualize the different worlds of ‘love,’ ‘shack,’ and ‘fancy,’ which is always how I think about my brand. That’s why there’s three perfumes. You can be all the different personas and moods and girls at the same time, or just one.”

Courtesy of LoveShackFancy

Courtesy of LoveShackFancy

Forever in Love, one of the three fragrances, embodies the brand’s love persona. It’s a fruity floral designed by Honorine Blanc of Firmenich and is anchored by a nostalgic green pear note along with gardenia, cedarwood, and jasmine. The shack fragrance is called Bohème, a classic floral designed by Yves Cassar of IFF, featuring notes of peony, jasmine, rose, and tuberose. Moondance, the fancy fragrance, is a warm floral designed by Frank Voelkl of Firmenich that highlights notes of velvet rose, Dreamwood, and orange flower. It is the most sophisticated, nighttime scent of the trio.

In true maximalist LoveShackFancy style, the bottles are vintage-inspired and designed with bow caps. “When I was in the markets in Paris, I was really searching for, like, the perfect inspiration for the bottle—something that felt nostalgic, feminine, and really like an heirloom piece that you could pass on,” Hessel Cohen tells us. The spray top can be removed to transform the whole thing into a bud vase when you’ve spritzed your way to the bottom.

Hessel Cohen carefully calls LoveShackFancy a lifestyle brand—not a clothing brand—because of how many categories it now covers. There’s clothing and accessories like sunglasses and shoes, options for babies and toddlers, and stationery, wallpaper, bedding, beach towels, and pool floats. “The clothes are definitely a vehicle into our world. And so now also being able to have beauty is another vehicle in the world,” she says. She notes that the fragrances are an accessible entry point into the signature girly aesthetic regardless of size, gender, or identity. “The fragrances will definitely bring out that feminine spirit in whoever it is. Anyone who wants to have that essence and spirit and be a part of that LoveShackFancy world—this is definitely the place for them.”

Courtesy of LoveShackFancy

LoveShackFancy founder and creative director Rebecca Hessel Cohen poses with a bottle of one of the brand’s three new fragrances, Forever in Love.

In the year of the Barbie movie, there’s been more than enough discourse about the revival of hyper-femininity and the subversive power of pink. And though I doubt anyone could take offense with these easy-to-love floral perfumes, LoveShackFancy sits squarely in the center of this hurricane. I ask Hessel Cohen whether she finds that description of her brand fair. “I think if they say, ‘Oh gosh, I could never wear that’ or ‘I don’t know if I could do LoveShackFancy,’ I’m like, ‘Well, have you tried it? Have you felt how it feels to wear it?’” she says. “There’s a sense of allure, and beauty, and femininity—it’s a sexiness that is appropriate. And it’s about just sort owning that and the confidence I think that comes across when you do.”

She notes that the brand recently added tailored pants (at the request of some of her Wall Street clients), and that you can find blazers, sexy black slipdresses, and plenty of lace to shop, too. “Even though it might seem like it’s all just pink, frilly, ruffled—there is really something for everyone.” But, she allows: “LoveShackFancy is definitely a mood and an energy and something to lean into in terms of a feeling. You kind of just need to let yourself go. Like, have a dance party and blast the music.”

Forever in Love, Bohème, and Moondance are available starting today in LoveShackFancy stores, online, and on sephora.com (and in Sephora stores September 15). And while the brand has had partnerships with Kiss nails and Supergoop! in the past, Hessel Cohen says this is just the beginning of its beauty journey. “LoveShackFancy’s a sisterhood—it’s about girls being together, and it’s about this kind of bond and love and fun, spirited energy,” she says. “So that’s definitely where we wanted to start, and we’re working on much more.”
