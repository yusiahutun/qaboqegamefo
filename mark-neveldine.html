Born and raised in Watertown, New York, United States, Mark Neveldine is a burgeoning American film director, film producer, screenwriter, and camera operator. He is best known for his work in movies like Crank, Crank II: High Voltage, and Ghost Rider: Spirit of Vengeance. Neveldine is also well recognized for his collaboration with director Brian Taylor as Neveldine/Taylor. He is happily married to actress, Alison Lohman. The couple is proud parents of two children.

Early Life

Mark Neveldine was born on May 11, 1973, in Watertown, New York, United States. He is the only son of Tom Neveldine and Carolyn Dowd Fitzpatrick. He is American and belongs to the White-American ethnicity.

Passionate about movies and theatre since childhood Neveldine attended Hobart and William Smith Colleges to pursue his career in drama and screenwriting, where he graduated in drama and psychology.

Career

Soon after his graduation, Mark Neveldine moved to Manhattan alone. He acted, wrote and directed for nearly thirty productions. Later, he found his interest in off-camera activities and enrolled in cinematography and worked on several music videos, documentaries, and a television pilot.

Neveldine successful directorial venture was the Ghost Rider: Spirit of Vengeance, the reboot of the Marvel Comics' superhero film Ghost Rider starring actor Nicolas Cage which he worked along with partner Brian Taylor and it earned $132.6 million in the box office.

He then officially collaborated with director Brian Taylor and professionally they are known as Neveldine & Taylor. Together they directed and wrote various films including Crank, its sequel, Crank: High Voltage, Pathology, Gamer and so on. The film critic, Armond White praised their collaboration as "Avant-garde" meaning new experimental ideas and methods in music and art.

Personal Life

Mark Neveldine is a married man, he exchanged vows with his longtime girlfriend, actress Alison Lohman. Their nuptials were held on August 19, 2009, in Watertown, New York at St. Anthony's Catholic Church.

Neveldine and his wife, Lohman is blessed with two children. They welcomed their first baby in 2010, a son, Billy Neveldine and after three years they welcomed their second child.
