All American: Daniel Ezra reveals his pet peeve about fan encounters

Publish date: 2024-08-14

He's quickly becoming a household name after being cast as rising NFL star Spencer James on Stan Exclusive series All American.

And British actor Daniel Ezra has revealed that while he loves interacting with fans, there is one downside of being famous.

Speaking to Daily Mail Australia, the 29-year-old said he doesn't like it when people just take out their phones and snap photos of him without asking. 

Introvert: British actor Daniel Ezra, the star of Stan Exclusive series All American, has revealed that while he loves interacting with fans, there is one downside of being famous 

'For me, the thing I always get the most weird about is if [fans] don't say anything and they just pull their phones out,' he said.

'I mean, I would much prefer [they say], "Let's take a picture, let's have a conversation."'

Daniel continued: 'If you're just standing next to me and the phone's there, I just freeze up. I'm so introverted, I don't know what to do.' 

Uncomfortable: Speaking to Daily Mail Australia, the 29-year-old said he doesn't like it when people just take out their phones and snap photos of him without asking

He hastened to add that one of the perks of fame is when younger fans mistake him for his character in All American. 

'Most of the time it's fine, it's usually young children and it's really sweet. They think I'm the character in real life,' he said with a laugh.   

Inspired by the life of retired NFL linebacker Spencer Paysinger, All American follows the story of a rising football star (Daniel) from South Los Angeles who is recruited to play for Beverly Hills High.

Synopsis: Inspired by the life of retired NFL linebacker Spencer Paysinger, All American follows the story of a rising football star (Daniel) from South Los Angeles who is recruited to play for Beverly Hills High

With season three now streaming on Stan in Australia, Daniel said the show's global popularity came as a 'shock' to him.

'I always believed in its potential, but the fanfare that came with it was a shock. It was an amazing thing to see, and it took its time to build into this phenomenon it has become,' he said.

'For a lot of [the cast] it was our first big thing to be a part of. We were all relatively new to acting in some way or at least at this level, so we were all blown away by it.'
