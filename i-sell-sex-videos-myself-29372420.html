"I just don't show my vagina, but I'll make videos of me using toys, but you can't like see my vagina, so I do a lot," she said.

Tara made headlines earlier this year as she revealed she'd had sexual relations with a female OnlyFans creator as part of a video collaboration, despite having a boyfriend.

She said her boyfriend "didn't care" she'd had relations with someone else, though.

Despite her boyfriend seemingly not bothered, it seems like many other people were after Tara made some comments on the situation.

As she was getting ready for the OnlyFans recording, she made a TikTok video saying that she likes to kiss women even though she dates men and is heterosexual.

"People took that as, like, [meaning that] I fetishise lesbians and [that] I have internalised homophobia," she said.

This isn't the first time that the content creator has been involved in a scandal, though.

She only returned to social media this year after another situation took place back in November last year.

Tara attracted backlash when she got a pet bird, Marty, and Googled to see whether, if she were to pet the animal's back, it would sexually stimulate them.

After doing this, she claimed as a joke that she had a sexual relationship with her bird.

The backlash also continued when she spent thousands of dollars on five Harry Styles concerts and said that she "finna be in the pit" in one of her TikTok clips.

"Finna" is an African-American Vernacular English (AAVE) word that is a contraction of the phrase "fixing to". It essentially meaning "going to" or "intending to." Many people said that her use of the AAVE term was akin to cultural appropriation.

Talking about the situation, she said: "All I thought about every day was how to defend myself, but it doesn't do anything.

"But it's all I would think about [...] and I was so f**ked up over it."
