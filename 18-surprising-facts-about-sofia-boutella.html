Sofia Boutella is an Algerian-French actress and dancer.

Born on April 6, 1982, in Bab El Oued, Algeria, Boutella later moved to Paris, France, where she honed her skills in both acting and dancing.

She started her career as a modern dancer.

Before entering the world of acting, Boutella made a name for herself as a professional modern and hip-hop dancer. She has performed with several prominent artists, including Madonna, Rihanna, and Michael Jackson.

Sofia Boutella gained international recognition for her role in the film “Kingsman: The Secret Service” (2014).

Her captivating performance as the lethal assassin Gazelle garnered praise from both critics and audiences alike, establishing her as a rising star in Hollywood.

She is known for her incredible dance skills.

Boutella’s background in dance has contributed to her unique style and fluidity of movement, setting her apart in both her acting and dancing performances.

Sofia Boutella played the role of Jaylah in “Star Trek Beyond” (2016).

Her portrayal of the fierce and resourceful alien earned her further acclaim and solidified her presence in the sci-fi genre.

She starred as the titular character in the film “The Mummy” (2017).

Boutella’s portrayal of the ancient princess Ahmanet showcased her versatility as an actress and her ability to bring complex characters to life.

Sofia Boutella has collaborated with renowned director Gaspar Noé.

She starred in Noé’s films “Climax” (2018) and “Lux Æterna” (2019), displaying her willingness to take on challenging and provocative roles.

She is fluent in multiple languages.

In addition to her native language Arabic and French, Boutella is also fluent in English and Spanish, allowing her to work in a diverse range of film industries.

Sofia Boutella was a semi-finalist on the French version of “Dancing with the Stars” (2012).

Her dance background certainly proved advantageous as she showcased her talents and grace on the popular dance competition show.

She appeared in the music video for Michael Jackson’s song “Hollywood Tonight” (2011).

Boutella’s dance skills were on full display in the visually stunning video, capturing the essence of Jackson’s iconic style.

Sofia Boutella plays the role of Clarisse McClellan in the HBO television adaptation of “Fahrenheit 451” (2018).

Her portrayal of the free-spirited informant in Ray Bradbury’s dystopian world showcases her ability to dive into thought-provoking and complex characters.

She has been recognized for her fashion sense.

Boutella has become a style icon, known for her daring red carpet looks and impeccable fashion choices.

Sofia Boutella starred alongside Jodie Foster in the science fiction film “Hotel Artemis” (2018).

Her performance as the no-nonsense and enigmatic assassin Nice further demonstrated her range as an actress.

She portrayed the role of Princess Ahmanet in “The Mummy” (2017).

Boutella’s portrayal of the iconic female monster garnered attention and praise from fans of the horror genre.

Sofia Boutella’s breakout role was in the dance film “StreetDance 2” (2012).

Her electrifying dance routines and undeniable screen presence catapulted her career and opened doors to more prominent roles.

She has collaborated with renowned fashion brands.

Boutella has been the face of campaigns for brands such as Nike and Reebok, merging her love for dance and fashion.

Sofia Boutella played the role of Delphine Lasalle in “Atomic Blonde” (2017).

Her performance as a French spy captured the attention of audiences and solidified her place in the action genre.

She embraces diverse and empowering roles.

Throughout her career, Boutella has actively sought out roles that challenge societal norms and empower women, making her an inspiration to many.
