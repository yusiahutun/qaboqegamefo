If Mary-Louise Parker is trying to show a different side to herself her current role should do the job.

The actor was spotted on the set of Parental Guidance Suggested, wearing an amazing outfit that really showed off her versatility.

The 48-year-old headed from the hair and make-up trailer in a gold swimsuit that revealed her well shaped body, yesterday, in Los Angeles.

Change of style: Mary-Louise Parker dressed head to toe in gold during filming of Parental Guidance Suggested, in Los Angeles, yesterday

The former star of Weeds looked a million miles away from her natural style as she dressed up like a very sexy bull fighter.

The mother-of-two sported a cropped blonde wig and wore a low cut gold swimsuit with a small bit of material tied around her waist that showed off her legs and toned torso.

She teamed the raunchy outfit with a pair of wedges and donned some gladiator-style shin guards that looked more for fashion than protection.

Hot thing: The actor looked amazing dressed as a sexy matador for her role in the movie

Bull fighter: Mary-Louise was able to show off her good legs in the gold swimsuit as she left the hair and make-up trailer

To complete the outfit Mary-Louise put on a sparkly gold and red matador jacket which featured large shoulder pads.

Parental Guidance Suggested co-stars teen sensation Selena Gomez and is an adaptation of Ric Browde's 2000 novel While I'm Dead ... Feed the Dog.

Parker plays a character called Lucy Stevens but also has a role as bullfighter Saint Lola.

Relaxed look: he 'Weeds' star worked out with her son William last week

Starring alongside Austin Stowell and Natt Wolff, Gomez will play Nina Pennington, and the offbeat coming-of-age comedy will centre around 16-year-old Ric Thibault (Wolff)  and his girlfriend (Gomez) who ends up in the back of a limousine on the road to Rock ‘N’ Roll super-stardom.

The film, which was written by Tim Garrick and will be directed by him, also features Dylan McDermott and English actor Cary Elwes.

Pretty in pink: Singer Selena Gomez grabbed some lunch during a break in filming, last week on the set of Parental Guidance Suggested

While Parker is working hard to make the transition from the small screen she will still be making money from her time spent on Weeds.

With the syndication system on US television, any show that airs for 100 episodes is guaranteed to make senior cast, writers and producers very wealthy.

In 2010, TV Guide Network bought the airing rights to Weeds, meaning episodes of the show will go into re-runs for a long time to come, resulting in a financial bounty continuing for Weeds cast and creators well after filming finally wraps.
