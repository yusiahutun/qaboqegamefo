Alicia Keys dons denim-on-denim look as she steps out in NYC despitehazardous smog from wildfires

Publish date: 2024-09-21

Alicia Keys was all smiles as she stepped out in New York City on Wednesday, before the harsh smog began smothering the area.

The 42-year-old songstress — who visited Cabo San Lucas last month — donned a triple denim look for a performance on Good Morning America, where she sang her famous hit song Like You'll Never See Me Again. 

Alicia, who is married to producer-entrepreneur Swizz Beatz, wore high-waisted light wash overalls with a denim bustier.

She flashed the top of her toned midriff and the ensemble was topped with a cropped jean jacket.

The mother-of-two was seen out without a protective mask although the metropolis is currently the most polluted city in the world as its Air Quality Index score soared past 200 due to Canadian wildfires.

She added a pair of silver-toned, square-shaped hoop earrings and dazzled in a face of soft makeup.

The Girl on Fire hitmaker pulled her lengthy, dark locks into a high-set, side ponytail of two-strand twists.

The superstar smiled and waved to a crowd of fans as she made her way to a black luxury SUV.

Keys, who rose to fame in the early 2000s with her sultry music, was a guest on Good Morning America on Wednesday.

One caption shared on the morning show's Instagram account read: '@aliciakeys is booked and busy! Not only is she kicking off a world tour on June 28, but she also just announced the world premiere of her original stage musical, “Hell’s Kitchen,” in the fall!'

She is currently promoting her Keys to the Summer Tour, which she has called her 'first ever 360 tour.'

Alicia's NYC outing comes as more than a dozen US states and over 100 million people are under air quality alerts as the smoke bellows over the nation.

Health experts have warned that breathing the fog can be as damaging as smoking six cigarettes.

At least ten school districts in New York cancelled all outdoor activities and vulnerable are being advised to limit outdoor exposure.

Side effects from the smog include irritation to the eyes and throat, alongside breathing problems.
