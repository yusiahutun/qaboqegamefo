Toffee the puppy pulled out of 60ft hole after more than 31 hours thanks to sardines

Publish date: 2024-09-21

Toffee, the seven-week-old pup, has been saved! 

The Australian shepherd mix was lifted out of a 60-ft crevice in Huntsville, Alabama, just before 12.30am on Saturday morning. 

As soon as the deaf pup got a breath of fresh air, it was immediately loved on and taken towards it's foster owner's home to be greeted by cameramen. 

The crowd cheered loudly once Toffee was taken out of the ground.

'My baby foster puppy Toffee fell in a very deep hole yesterday,' Collins West wrote on Facebook. 'We can see her when she is walking around and whining.

'Please pray and let me know if you have any ideas I am devastated.' 

West shared with WHNT that she had been unaware of the hole before the dog fell, believing it to be filled with rocks.  

A net with food was lowered but the puppy didn't get in. Water and ways to stay warm have also been sent down.

Huntsville television stations are live-streaming the recovery efforts.

Toffee is an Australian shepherd mix. She is deaf and because she is so young, her eyes have not open which has all but ruled out the usage of laser pointers to get her attention. 

A group called A New Leash on Life has started taking donations for the dog, gaining over $1,000 so far in a day.
