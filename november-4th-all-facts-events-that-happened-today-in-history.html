HISTORICAL EVENTS

  • 1979: The Iran hostage crisis began, as Iranian militants seized the U.S. Embassy in Tehran, holding 52 hostages for 444 days.
  • 1922: Howard Carter discovered the entrance to the tomb of Tutankhamun in the Valley of the Kings, Egypt.
  • 1862: American Civil War: United States President Abraham Lincoln removes George B. McClellan as commander of the Union Army.
  • 1956: The Hungarian Revolution of 1956 against Soviet rule begins, eventually leading to a Soviet crackdown and the execution of Imre Nagy.
  • 2008: Barack Obama becomes the first African-American to be elected President of the United States.

SCIENTIFIC BREAKTHROUGHS

  • 1922: British archaeologist Howard Carter discovers the burial mask of Pharaoh Tutankhamun.
  • 1924: Nellie Ross becomes the first female governor in the United States when she is elected Governor of Wyoming.
  • 1952: The United States successfully detonates the first hydrogen bomb, codenamed “Ivy Mike,” at the Eniwetok Atoll in the Marshall Islands.
  • 1957: The Soviet Union launches Sputnik 2, carrying the first living creature, a dog named Laika, into space.
  • 2004: The European Space Agency’s Rosetta spacecraft successfully completes its flyby of Earth, using the planet’s gravity to propel itself towards its destination, Comet 67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko.

POLITICAL MILESTONES

  • 2008: Barack Obama is elected as the 44th President of the United States, becoming the first African-American to hold the office.
  • 1948: Harry S. Truman defeats Thomas E. Dewey in the U.S. presidential election, defying predictions and polls.
  • 1980: Ronald Reagan is elected as the 40th President of the United States, defeating incumbent President Jimmy Carter.
  • 1979: Iranian militants seize the U.S. Embassy in Tehran, initiating the Iran hostage crisis.
  • 1956: Hungarian Revolution against Soviet rule breaks out, leading to a Soviet crackdown and the execution of Imre Nagy.
