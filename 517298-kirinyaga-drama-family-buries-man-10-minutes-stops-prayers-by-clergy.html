Kirinyaga: Puzzle as Family Buries Man Within 10 Minutes, Stops Prayers by Clergy

Publish date: 2024-06-11
  • The burial of 60-year-old Stanley Mugo surprised villagers in Kirinyaga after it took place in under 10 minutes without prayers
  • James Ngaire, a relative, revealed that Mugo had expressed his desire to skip any ceremonial activities during his burial
  • John Mugo Nyamu, the chairman of Mugo's burial committee, said they also had unanswered questions about why Mugo chose to be buried in such an unconventional manner

Kirinyaga - Residents of Mwangathia village in Ndia Constituency were left in shock after a family rushed to bury their kin.

Most African traditions hold the dead in high regard and, in most cases, always keep a vigil or have a prayer service before burial.

However, as shocked villagers watched, 60-year-old Stanley Mugo's burial happened in less than 10 minutes.

James Ngaire, a relative of Mugo, disclosed that he was against any ceremony being conducted during his burial.

Ngaire said he wished to have his body taken directly from the mortuary and into his last resting ground.

"We took the body from the mortuary and took it directly to the grave as he wished," Ngaire said, as reported by Citizen Digital.

Why Mugo wanted to be buried this way

The chairman of Mugo's burial committee, John Mugo Nyamu, explained the last respects to the deceased could be expressed in various ways among Africans.

Nyamu said they also had many unanswered questions on why Mugo opted to be buried in an unconventional manner.

"Last respect for the departed is expressed in so many ways, mostly the religion or traditional practices, but we wonder why Mugo decided to be buried this way," Mugo opined.

During the burial, a moment of confusion occurred when a group of local clergymen tried to conduct prayers.
