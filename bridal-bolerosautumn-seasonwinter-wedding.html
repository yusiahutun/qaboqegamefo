Bridal bolero jackets: this autumn, worship the cold

Publish date: 2024-02-21

    There are so many pros to getting married in the autumn: dream settings, warm ranges of colour and romantic images. But what are the cons? The drop in temperature from September onwards and the cold winds can deter many brides from wearing low cut open back dresses or even exposed arms on their big day.

    But why give up on the dress of your dreams for the simple matter of weather? You just have to look at some of the latest trends on the runways to see how to avoid worrying about the wavering temperatures.

    Boleros, short coats or even cardigans can be encrusted with precious stones to create a look that is perfect for the autumn bride without needing furs, capes or even overcoats.

    Alternatively you can use an overgarment in such a way as to update the traditional bridal look.

    With styles that include short cap sleeves, built in gloves, lace embroidery, silk sleeves and frills, designers have come up with a thousand and one ways to forget the cold on your wedding day.
