Robert Davi - Bio, Age, Height, Net Worth, Facts, Nationality

Table of Biography

Robert Davi is a multi-talented famed actor, singer, writer, and producer from the United States. Robert Davi is widely known for playing FBI Special Agent Bailey Malone on NBC’s “Profiler.”

Early Life

Robert Davi was born on the 26th of June 1953, in Astoria, Queens, New York, the United States of America. His birth name is Robert John Davi and he holds an American nationality. Currently, he is 70 years old and has a birth sign of Cancer. Meanwhile, he belongs to a mixed ethnicity and his religious beliefs are unknown at the moment.

He was born to his parents; Sal Davi (father) and Maria Davi (mother). His mother was an Italian American whose family originated in Nusco, Avellino, and Campania, and his father in Torretta, Palermo, Sicily. During his childhood days, he speaks the Italian language. He grew up with his two sisters whose names are Yvonne Davi (deceased) and Michelle Queal.

Robert Davi – Education

Meanwhile, he went to a Roman Catholic high school named Seton Hall in New York. Later, he enrolled at Hofstra University which was renowned for its strong drama department. He also studied acting with Stella Adler, the legendary acting coach.

Professional Career

Robert Davi made his acting debut in the 1977 television film “Contract on Cherry Street” along with Frank Sinatra. Coming up next, he appeared in the 1978 television series “Charlie’s Angels” playing the role of Ritchie in an episode of ‘Mother Angel.’

Meanwhile, he appeared in several television series from the late 1970s to the early 1980s including television series such as “The Incredible Hulk,” “Barnaby Jones,” “Trapper John, M.D.,” “Dynasty,” “St. Elsewhere,” “The Powers of Matthew Star,” “The Optimist,” “The Fall Guy,” and “The A-Team.”

During this period, he also appeared in TV films such as “The Legend of the Golden Gun” (1979), “And Alcatraz: The Whole Shocking Story” (1980), as well as TV miniseries like “From Here to Eternity” (1979),  and “The Gangster Chronicles” (1981).

Continued working successfully in the entertainment industry

In 1984, he made his film debut in the buddy-crime-comedy film “City Heat.” His other film credits include “Die Hard” (1988), where he portrayed the villainous henchman Hans Gruber; “Licence to Kill” (1989), in which he played the main antagonist, Franz Sanchez; and “The Goonies” (1985), where he portrayed one of the Fratelli brothers.

Caption: The poster of NBC’s television series, Profiler (Source: IMDb)

Additionally, he played the role of  deputy chief Phil Heinemann in the 1990 film “Predator 2.” He played the role of strip club manager Al Torres in the 1995 erotic thriller film “Showgirls.” In the NBC television series “Profiler,” he portrayed FBI Special Agent Bailey Malone.

He ended the decade with appearances in the TV series “VR.5” and “Batman Beyond.” Moreover, he lent the  voice to the character Colonel Juan Garcia Cortez in the 2002 video game series “Grand Theft Auto: Vice City.” He played Stan in “The Hot Chick,” which is a teen comedy film.

Direction debut and other acting works

He made his directorial debut with the 2007 comedy-drama film “The Dukes.” The cast included Davi, Peter Bogdanovich, and Chazz Palminteri. It had its world premiere at the Rome Film Festival. In the years since, he has appeared in films such as “Kill the Irishman,” “Swamp Shark,” “The Iceman,” and “Blood of Redemption.”

Some of his recent movie appearances are “Sicilian Vampire” (2015), “Criminal” (2016), “Mob Town” (2019), and “Roe v. Wade” (2020). Meanwhile, he also appeared in the reality television series “Hell’s Kitchen” in the year 2015. One of his recent television roles was in the “Paper Empire” (2021-2022) playing Lawrence Fintch in 11 episodes.

Singer

Caption: Robert Davi singing his way through the Frank Sinatra songbook (Source: Long Island Weekly)

Apart from acting, he is also a well-known singer. Davi started his singing career in 2011, working with “Voice Builder” Gary Catona as he prepared to record his first children’s album. His debut album, Davi Sings Sinatra — On The Road To Romance, peaked at number six on the Billboard Jazz charts. Davi praised for his voice, made his debut as a headliner at The Venetian in Las Vegas, Nevada, six months after the record was released. Meanwhile, he was the guest singer on the Italian version of the TV program “The Voice.”

Awards

As a multi-talent person, he has achieved huge success in his career. In 2007, he won two Special Jury Awards at WorldFest Houston, two Jury Prizes at Monte-Carlo Comedy Film Festival, and two Queens Spirit Awards at Queens Film Festival. Similarly, he won the 2008 Coup de Coeur at the Alpe d’Huez International Comedy Film Festival. In the 2020 Chandler International Film Festival, he won the Career Achievement Award. Meanwhile, he was enshrined in Toronto’s Italian Walk of Fame in 2013.
