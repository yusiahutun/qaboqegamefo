Career and Professional Life

Moving on to her career, Gillian Jacobs made her acting debut at the age of 16 as Titania in the stage adaptation of Shakespeare’s “A Midsummer Night’s Dream”. Years later, she made her feature film debut in Building Girl before making her television debut as Adele Congreve in three episodes of The Book of Daniel.

Moreover, she appeared in the off-Broadway production “Cagelove” in 2006. The play received negative feedback from all quarters, but all reviews praised her acting ability. She has also appeared in plays such as The Fabulous Life of a Size Zero in 2007 and The Little Flower of East Orange in 2008.

Furthermore, in March 2009, she was cast as Britta Perry, a high school dropout aspiring to be a psychologist, on the NBC single-camera comedy series Community. On September 16, 2014, it was announced that she had been cast as Mickey in the Netflix original comedy series Love, which aired from February 2016 to March 2018.

In addition, she also co-starred in Janicza Bravo’s first full-length feature, Lemon, which premiered at the Sundance Film Festival in 2017. Later, she also directed a narrated short film Curated in 2018. Besides that, she appeared in Kris Rey’s comedy-drama film I Used to Go Here in 2020.

In July 2021, she appeared as Christine “Ziggy” Berman in the Netflix horror film The Fear Street Trilogy. In 2022, she will have both directing and acting credits on a feature film “The Seven Faces of Jane”.
